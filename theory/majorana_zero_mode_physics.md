# Majorana Zero Mode Physics

Majorana zero modes are zero-energy boundary states in topological superconductors. They are their own antiparticles.

## Key Physics
- Emerge in semiconductor nanowires with strong spin-orbit coupling, proximity-induced s-wave superconductivity, and external magnetic field (Kitaev chain generalization).
- Described effectively by Ising anyon statistics.
- Exchanging two Majorana zero modes performs a non-abelian operation on the degenerate ground state.
- Experimental signatures observed in various platforms, but definitive demonstration of non-abelian statistics remains an active research frontier.

## Connection to Codex
Provides a physically accessible route to protected braiding operations. Maps to the Ising layer of topological order — simpler and more experimentally grounded than Fibonacci, while still offering topological protection for memory-like operations.
